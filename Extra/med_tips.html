CPR Instructions
(Cardiopulmonary resuscitation)


Materials Needed

  • Two functioning hands
  • A functioning mouth
  • No shame (Most important)

Step 1

Assess the situation. Make sure the person is in need of help. You can do so by tapping their shoulder and asking ," Are you okay?" If it is apparent that he or she is in danger, you or someone else call 911.

Step 2

Open the airway of the person in danger. Do this by laying the person on their back and tilt their head back to lift the chin up. If you do not head the person breathe for five seconds, start to begin CPR. Irregular gasping for air does not mean they are fine.

Step 3

Place your hands on top of each other, in the middle of the person's chest. Use your body weight to help you to push fast and hard. Each compression should be at least two inches deep.

Step 4

Start to deliver rescue breathes. Tilt the person's head down slightly to lift their chin up and pinch their nose shut. Blow into the person's mouth so their chest rises.

Step 5

Repeat steps of chest compressions and breathing until the person displays signs of life. You should do 100 chest compressions then rescue breaths. Continue until an AED becomes availiable or a trained medical responder arrives on the scene.
